My Thesis

My undergraduate thesis, "Data Discovery and Data Exploration in Data Lakes", looks into ways to find useful patterns in complex, varied data stores. I'm using a mix of semantic and syntactic analysis, plus some AI techniques, to improve how we extract and clean data. By experimenting with different approaches, I hope to make it easier for organizations to tap into the potential of their data lakes.

Projects

One of my recent projects focuses on mapping public election data in Uruguay. Using Python, geospatial libraries like GeoPandas, and Folium, I combined official election results with geographic details at the municipality level. This let me create an interactive map where users can:

  • View how different regions voted, seeing patterns across the country.
  • Explore results at a granular level, making data more engaging than static charts.

This project shows my ability to connect data sources, process them efficiently, and present the results in a way that's easier to understand and explore.
